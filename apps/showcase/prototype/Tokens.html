Wylie Dog Field Notes · Issue 14
8 min read

Engineering · April 2026

Why we rebuilt the entire color system in a week.

Ten months of hex codes, three product redesigns, and one paint-bucket migration later — here is what an OKLCH-native token system actually buys you.

EM

Elena Mori

Staff design engineer · @ewmori

Last March we shipped a redesign of the dashboard. Three weeks later, accessibility filed forty-one tickets on contrast alone. The hex codes we'd been hand-tuning since 2023 collapsed the moment we tried to extend the system into a dark mode that wasn't just an inverted afterthought.

The fix wasn't another paint job. It was abandoning hex altogether and rebuilding every color in oklch() — a perceptually uniform color space where lightness, chroma, and hue mean what they say. One step on the lightness axis is one perceptual step. No more guessing whether 500 and 600 will read as different shades.

"We stopped designing colors and started designing relationships between them."

The semantic tier is where the magic actually lives. Components never reach for --color-violet-500 directly. They consume --color-interactive-primary, and the cascade re-resolves itself in dark mode, in high-contrast, in any future theme we haven't designed yet.
